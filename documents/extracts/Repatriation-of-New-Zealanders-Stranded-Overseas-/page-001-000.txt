Repatriation of New Zealanders Stranded Overseas

The group of Ministers with Power to Act on COVID-19 matters [CAB-20-MIN-0130] convened

on 16 April 2020 at 10.30am, and in accordance with their Power to Act:

1 noted that hundreds of thousands of New Zealanders remain offshore and that a demand for
government-assisted repatriation could come from between one and several thousand of
them affected by the COVID-19 emergency;

2 noted that:

2.1 New Zealand Government repatriations carried out to date have been expensive and
resource intensive;

2.2 the government cannot bring home all New Zealanders who wish to return;

2.3 with finite resources, there is a need to prioritise future repatriations|s9(2)(f)(iv), $9(2)(g)(i)

3 noted that the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade (MFAT) is applying a repatriations
framework that:

3.1 categorises countries where New Zealanders are located as being high risk, medium
risk, and low risk;
3.2 deploys the most effective and resource-efficient transport options available;
