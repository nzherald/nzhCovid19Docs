COVID-19 Antarctic Issues: Providing Emergency and Essential

Support to Antarctic Stations Through New Zealand

The group of Ministers with Power to Act on COVID-19 matters [CAB-20-MIN-0130] convened

on 3 April 2020 at 10.30am, and in accordance with their Power to Act:

Essential support to Antarctic stations

1 noted that in March 2020, Cabinet directed the Ministry of Health and the Ministry of
Foreign Affairs and Trade to provide further advice to Ministers on issues concerning
Antarctica travel [CVD-20-MIN-0017] ;

2 noted that the Ministry of Health has confirmed that there are currently no COVID-19
related risks associated with people coming from Antarctica, and that COVID-19 is not
present in Antarctica;

3 noted that as an Antarctic ‘gateway’ country, New Zealand has an essential role in
supporting Antarctic stations in the Ross Sea region;

4 noted that April is a critical month for preparing Antarctic stations for the winter;

5 noted that provision for essential and emergency support to Antarctic stations through
New Zealand is required as part of New Zealand’s Antarctic responsibilities, as well as to
keep New Zealanders in Antarctica safe, and can be delivered whilst preventing increased
COVID-19 related risks to people in New Zealand or in Antarctica;

6 noted that:

6.1 all personnel proposed to return from Antarctica to New Zealand, as part of the
movements described in the paper COVID-19 Antarctic Issues: Providing
Emergency and Essential Support to Antarctic Stations Through New Zealand, will
go into self-funded self-isolation on arrival in New Zealand;

6.2 for the foreign nationals, this will be until such time as they are evacuated from New
Zealand (and in accordance with any requirements for the evacuation of foreign
nationals) or the 14 days have passed, after which they would be required to comply
with the alert level 4 restrictions;

6.3 plans for self-isolation will be scrutinised before arrival in New Zealand and
government officials, including from the Ministry of Health, will also need to be
satisfied as to the adequacy of the proposed self-isolation arrangements upon arrival;
