COVID-19: Repatriation Flights: A Framework for Decision-making on

the Repatriation of Foreign Nationals

The group of Ministers with Power to Act on COVID-19 matters [CAB-20-MIN-0130] convened

on 27 March 2020 at 10.30am, and in accordance with their Power to Act:

1 noted that New Zealanders overseas and foreign nationals in New Zealand are being
encouraged to ‘shelter in place’ unless they have secured arrangements to depart, but that
options for assisted departures are being kept under review;

2 agreed that no assisted departures of foreign nationals in New Zealand should occur for the
remainder of March 2020;

3 directed officials to report back to COVID-19 Ministers by 31 March 2020 with a
framework for considering foreign governments’ requests to approve and support
repatriation flights, including domestic travel requirements;

4 agreed that the key principles in operationalising managed departures by foreign nationals
that would need to be met include:

4.] managed departures are assessed not to increase the burden on the New Zealand

~ public health system;

4.2 any movements must be managed in a way that minimises the risk of the further
spread of COVID-19 within New Zealand;

4.3 resources could be allocated by the New Zealand government to manage assisted
departures without taking away from higher priorities;

4.4 foreign governments can prove that all health and logistical arrangements are in
place and to the satisfaction of relevant New Zealand authorities;

5 agreed that foreign governments be advised to suspend planning for government chartered
repatriation flights departing New Zealand before 1 April 2020 and until such time as a
framework is in place;
