COVID-19 Support for Essential Social Sector Services and

Communities

The group of Ministers with Power to Act on COVID-19 matters [CAB-20-MIN-0130] convened

on 26 March 2020 at 10.30pm and, in accordance with their Power to Act:

I noted that the Ministry of Social Development and Oranga Tamariki have publicly released.
guidance on what is an essential social service for the purpose of the government’s COVID-
19 response Alert Level 4;

2 noted that non-government organisations and communities provide essential services that
will need to continue now the Government has moved to COVID-19 Alert Level 4;

4. agreed to $16 million to ensure that essential social sector services continue to operate and
can meet increased demand during Alert Level 4;

5: agreed to $6 million to support disability sector providers to change how they deliver
essential services during COVID-19 Alert Level 4;

6. agreed to $4.8 million to provide community grants or fund innovative community-led
solutions to support local resilience;

ds note that the Ministry of Social Development will be working to ensure that any services
funded are taking steps to ensure that maximum hygiene protocols are in place for essential
workers;

8. noted that due to the unprecedented nature of COVID-19 and the uncertainty around its
impacts over the coming months it is prudent to create a flexible funding mechanism to
support communities;
