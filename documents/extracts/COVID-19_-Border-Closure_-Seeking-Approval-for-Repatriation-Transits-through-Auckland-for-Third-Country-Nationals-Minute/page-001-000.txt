COVID-19: Border Closure: Seeking Approval for Repatriation Transits
through Auckland for Third Country Nationals
The group of Ministers with Power to Act on COVID-19 matters [CAB-20-MIN-0130] convened
on 22 March 2020 at 5.00 pm and, in accordance with their Power to Act:
1 noted that under current border settings third country nationals are unable to transit
New Zealand airports to be repatriated to home countries and that this is currently
inconsistent with Australian measures;
2 noted that this presents foreign policy risks and potentially negative implications for
New Zealand citizens seeking to transit foreign airports;
3 noted that in the Pacific, including the Realm, there are up to 700 third country nations
seeking to be repatriated via Auckland;
4 agreed to open a time-limited window of:
4.1 up to 1500 Tuesday 24 March 2020 (NZT) to enable transit via New Zealand to
Australia for onward connection:
4.1.1 from the Pacific;
4.1.2 to and from South America;
4.2 up to one week (Sunday 29 March 2020) to enable transit via New Zealand:
4.2.1 from Australia to the United States and Canada for US and Canadian
nationals
4.2.2 from the Pacific for other nationals not transiting Australia to their home
countries;
5 noted that transit of passengers will only be allowed if the following conditions are met in
order to manage the health risks to New Zealand:
5.1 no COVID-19 test result that is positive or for which the results have not been
received in the previous 14 days;
5.2. no close contact with a suspected or confirmed case in the previous 14 days;
